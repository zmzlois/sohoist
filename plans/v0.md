# Sohoist Product Scope Plan

## Product Thesis

**Sohoist** is a private introduction network for people who want to meet through trusted friends in real life.

Core positioning:

> Match by friends in real life.

**Product principles:**
- Private by default
- Referral-first, not swipe-first
- Low-pressure, high-context introductions
- Profiles feel like private intro briefs, not dating ads
- Rewards feel like gratitude, not transactions
- High-profile users need discretion, control, and plausible deniability

**Sohoist should feel like:**
- A private members club
- A personal introduction dossier
- A concierge matchmaking service
- A trusted friend network

**Sohoist should avoid feeling like:**
- A public dating marketplace
- A dating bounty board
- Sugar dating
- Betting / gambling
- A swipe app

---

## Core User Roles

### Member

The person who wants to be introduced.

- Create a private profile / intro brief
- Set privacy and visibility
- Upload photos and generate pencil sketches
- Invite trusted referrers
- Create a private reward pool
- Review, accept, or decline introductions
- Confirm relationship status

### Referrer / Friend

A trusted friend who can suggest matches.

- View the member's approved intro brief
- Submit thoughtful referrals
- Add context on why the match could work
- Track referral status
- Receive a reward if the intro becomes a verified relationship

### Candidate / Match

The person being introduced.

- Receive a tasteful private intro
- View a limited profile after approval
- Accept or decline the introduction
- Confirm relationship status if things progress

### Concierge / Admin

Internal operator for trust, moderation, and high-profile support.

- Review applications
- Approve members and referrers
- Handle privacy needs
- Review disputes
- Support concierge onboarding
- Manage circles and payouts

## Feature Specs

### 1. Login & Account Creation

**Sign in with:** phone · email · Google · Apple

**Basic setup:** name · age · city · relationship intent · gender / identity (optional)

**Privacy-first onboarding message:**
- Profile is private by default
- User controls who can view, refer, and receive intros

**Screens:** welcome → sign in / create account → privacy promise → start membership application

> Welcome to Sohoist.
>
> Private introductions through people you trust.
> Your profile is never public by default. You choose who can see it, who can refer, and when an intro is shared.

---

### 2. Private Membership Application

**Application form:** name · city · profession / background · relationship intent · why Sohoist

**Verification:** phone · email · ID verification (optional for MVP) · LinkedIn / professional profile (optional)

**Application states:** submitted → under review → approved → waitlisted → rejected

**Later:** invite code · nomination by existing member · concierge interview

**Verified circles:** founders · creatives · operators · city-based communities

> Apply privately.
>
> Sohoist is built for thoughtful introductions, not public browsing.

---

### 3. Voice AI Profile Creation

**Purpose:** use a voice agent to make profile creation human, fast, and low-friction.

**Voice interview prompts:**
1. Who are you?
2. What are you like in real life?
3. What kind of person do you enjoy being around?
4. What are you looking for?
5. What should friends know before referring someone?
6. What are your dealbreakers?

**AI generates:**
- Profile headline
- Short bio
- "Open to meeting"
- "Friends should refer someone who…"
- "Do not refer if…"
- Values / lifestyle tags
- Conversation starters
- Private notes for referrers

**User can:** edit · regenerate · shorten · make warmer · choose tone

**Screen states:** listening → thinking → draft generated → edit profile → confirm intro brief

> Let's get to know you.
>
> I'll help create a private intro brief that sounds like you.
> Not a dating profile. A better way for friends to introduce you.

---

### 4. Profile Voice / Rewrite Assistant

**Purpose:** help users present themselves tastefully without sounding awkward or desperate.

**AI suggests:** headline · short intro · tone variants

**Tone options:** warm · concise · witty · elegant · grounded · founder/operator style

**Feature names to use:** profile voice · intro tone · rewrite my brief · make this sound like me

**Avoid:** "rename yourself"

**Example outputs:**

| Tone | Example |
|---|---|
| Warm | Open to thoughtful introductions through people who know me well. |
| Editorial | Looking for real-life chemistry, shared values, and a good dinner conversation. |
| Low-key | Not actively on dating apps, but open to meeting the right person through friends. |
| Founder | Building a full life, and open to meeting someone who fits the rhythm of it. |

---

### 5. Photo Upload & Pencil Sketch Generation

**Purpose:** let users create beautiful, private, non-thirsty profile visuals.

**Flow:** upload photo → choose sketch style → generate portrait → review → set visibility

**Sketch styles:** soft graphite · editorial pencil · watercolor portrait · minimal line sketch

**Privacy controls — user chooses whether approved viewers see:**
- Sketch only
- Original photo
- Both
- Photo only after intro approval

Original photos should stay private by default.

> Turn your photo into a private pencil portrait.
>
> A softer way to be seen before an introduction.

---

### 6. Private Intro Brief

**Purpose:** replace the public dating profile with a refined private dossier.

**Required sections:**
- Pencil portrait
- Name / age / city
- Short bio
- Relationship intent
- Values / lifestyle tags
- What they are looking for
- What friends should know
- Suggested intro notes
- Visibility status
- Reward status (if enabled)

**Visibility tiers:**
- Hidden from search
- Approved referrers only
- Trusted circle only
- Candidate sees after approval
- Public preview (optional)

**Use:** "private intro brief" — avoid: "public profile", "dating profile", "bounty profile"

> Your private intro brief helps trusted friends make better introductions.

---

### 7. Ghost Mode & Privacy Controls

**Controls:**
- Hidden from search
- Visible only to approved referrers
- Candidate sees profile only after approval
- Hide exact reward amount
- Hide exact city
- Hide profession
- Hide activity / last active
- Revoke viewer access

**Preview what each audience sees:** referrer view · candidate view · public preview

> Ghost Mode
>
> You remain private until you choose to be introduced.

---

### 8. Trusted Referrers

**Purpose:** let members choose who can introduce them.

**Invite friends via:** contacts · phone · email · private link · QR code / native share sheet

**Referrer states:** invited → accepted → approved → removed

**Permissions:**
- View intro brief
- Submit referrals
- See reward status
- See relationship milestone updates

**Referrer profile fields:** name · relationship to member · trust status · intros made · referral quality score (later)

> Choose your trusted circle.
>
> These are the people who know your taste, your rhythm, and who you might actually click with.

---

### 9. Profile Sharing

**Sharing options:**
- Private invite link
- QR code
- Native share sheet
- Copy link
- Custom invitation message
- Expiring links (optional)

**Rules:** friend must sign in before viewing · user can revoke access

**Sharing modes:** invite as referrer · share candidate preview · share anonymous preview · share with trusted circle

**Default message:**
> I'm using Sohoist for private introductions. If someone comes to mind who you genuinely think I'd click with, I'd love your referral.

**High-profile message:**
> I'm privately open to thoughtful introductions. This link is only for people I trust to make considered referrals.

---

### 10. Private Reward Pool

**Purpose:** reward friends for meaningful introductions without making the product feel transactional.

**Reward options:** $100 · $250 · $500 · custom amount

**Deposit options:** minimum deposit · half deposit · full deposit

**Rules:** funds held in trust / escrow-like status · reward releases only after verified milestone · user can hide exact amount · user can show "reward funded" badge instead

**Language to use:** private intro reward · reward pool · held in trust · reward funded · thank-you reward

**Avoid:** bounty · cash prize · bet · marketplace

**Reward states:**
- Not created
- Reward active
- Partially funded
- Fully funded
- Intro accepted
- Relationship pending
- Eligible for payout
- Paid
- Refunded
- Disputed
- Expired

> Create a private intro reward.
>
> A small way to thank the person who makes a thoughtful introduction.

---

### 11. Referral Submission

**Referrer submits:**
- Candidate name
- Candidate contact
- Candidate city
- Why they are a fit
- How referrer knows them
- Optional voice note
- Optional photo / profile link
- Confidence level
- Whether candidate knows about the intro

**Quality prompts:**
- Why do you think they would click?
- What values or lifestyle do they share?
- Have they met before?
- Is this person currently single?
- Would you personally vouch for them?

**Referral states:**
- Draft → submitted → viewed → accepted → declined
- Candidate invited → candidate accepted → intro active
- First date logged → relationship confirmed → payout pending → paid

> Make a thoughtful introduction.
>
> Tell us why this person fits in real life — not just on paper.

---

### 12. Referral Inbox

**Filters:** new · reviewed · accepted · declined

**Candidate card:** sketch portrait · name / age / city · referred by · short reason · confidence

**Actions:** accept intro · decline · ask for more context · save for later

> Referral inbox
>
> Introductions from people who know you best.

---

### 13. Candidate Intro Experience

**Candidate receives:** tasteful intro message · who referred them · limited member profile · accept / decline option · privacy notice

**Candidate actions:** accept · decline · ask for more context · report concern

> Taylor thought you and Elena might enjoy meeting.
>
> No pressure. If this feels interesting, you can accept the introduction.

---

### 14. Intro Room

**Purpose:** once both sides accept, create a low-pressure private space.

**Required:**
- Shared intro page
- Referrer context
- Both limited profiles
- Suggested conversation starters
- Optional date planning
- Chat or contact exchange
- Safety / report controls

**Later:** suggest coffee/dinner spot · date availability · calendar integration · referrer intro note

> You've both accepted the intro.
>
> Start with context, not a cold message.

---

### 15. Relationship Verification

**Both parties can confirm:** met · dating · in a relationship · no longer seeing each other

**Support:** friend confirmations · optional proof upload if disputed · milestone reminders · six-month verification flow

**Verification states:**
- Intro accepted → first date confirmed → dating confirmed → relationship confirmed
- 3-month check-in → 6-month review → verified relationship → reward released

> Confirm relationship status.
>
> This helps protect everyone and ensures rewards are released fairly.

---

### 16. Escrow & Payout

**Required:**
- Show reward status
- Show deposit amount
- Show release conditions
- Show payout recipient
- Show payout timeline
- Handle refunds
- Handle disputes
- Require payout identity verification for referrers

**Release conditions — reward releases when:**
- The intro was accepted by both people
- The relationship is mutually confirmed
- The relationship remains active for 6 months
- No disputes are open

**Payout states:**
- Funds deposited → intro accepted → relationship confirmed
- Six-month review scheduled → verification pending → payout approved → payout sent
- Refunded / disputed

---

### 17. Concierge Review

**Purpose:** support high-profile users and preserve premium trust.

**Required:**
- Profile reviewed
- Intro preferences reviewed
- Privacy tier confirmed
- Reward settings reviewed
- Concierge notes

> Concierge Review

## Design Decisions (from /plan-design-review — 2026-05-16)

### Voice Profile Creation — Redesigned

**Decision:** Simplify from 6 questions to 2 open-ended prompts.

- Question 1: "Tell me about yourself." (free-form, 1–3 min)
- Question 2: "Tell me about who you're looking for." (free-form, 1–3 min)

**Screen states:**
1. **Before speaking** — large Cormorant Garamond italic question, centered. Soft fog-blue circle (thin pencil stroke) as tap target. "Tap to begin" label. "1 / 2" step counter. Ghost pill "Prefer to type instead →" at bottom.
2. **Listening** — circle breathes gently (expands on speech, contracts on pause). "Listening..." label. "Done" graphite pill appears.
3. **Between questions** — question 2 fades in. Step counter updates to "2 / 2".
4. **Reveal (after both questions)** — paper card slides up from bottom showing:
   - AI-generated summary/headline + 2-sentence bio
   - Full transcript of what the user said (collapsible, secondary)
   - "This sounds like me →" dark pill CTA
   - "Edit this" ghost secondary

**Approved mockup direction:** `~/.gstack/projects/wing-men.cc/designs/sohoist-mvp-20260516/voice-v3/variant-A.png`

---

### Reward Pool Creation — Redesigned

**Decision:** Envelope + wax seal motif. Trust framing, not financial framing.

**Screen layout:**
- Pencil-sketch envelope illustration at top third, wax seal in warm amber (#D6B56D)
- Headline: "Set your private reward." (Cormorant Garamond 30px, left-aligned)
- Amount selection: 3 horizontal cards — "$100" / "$250" / "$500" in IBM Plex Mono. Selected card has warm amber border. "Custom amount" text link below.
- No body copy. No marketing text.
- **Two CTAs:**
  - Primary (graphite pill, full width): "I trust you →"
  - Secondary (ghost pill, full width): "Test a small amount first →"
- "See terms of trust →" tiny link at bottom

**Rationale:** Two CTAs acknowledge different levels of trust readiness. "I trust you" addresses Sohoist as an entity. "Test a small amount first" removes shame from first-time hesitation.

**Approved mockup direction:** `~/.gstack/projects/wing-men.cc/designs/sohoist-mvp-20260516/reward-final/`

### Screens Requiring Design Before Build (next phase)

1. **Pencil sketch generation** — photo upload → AI generating (not a spinner — a pencil drawing animation) → sketch reveal. Brand moment.
2. **Ghost Mode controls** — privacy panel: visibility tier, who can see what, revoke referrer access, hide/show reward amount.

5. **Shareable profile** — the referrer's and candidate's view of the member dossier. Must match the design system. This is the whole pitch for inviting referrers.
